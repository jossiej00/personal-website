Computational biology has a reputation as one of the more open fields in science. We post preprints, share code on GitHub, deposit data in public repositories. Compared to clinical research or chemistry, we look pretty good.

But I've been thinking lately about where we fall short — and I think the picture is more mixed than the reputation suggests.

## What We Do Well

Preprints. The adoption of bioRxiv and arXiv in computational biology has been genuinely transformative. Results are available months or years before formal publication, peer review is decoupled from access, and the feedback loop between researchers has accelerated.

Data deposition. The major journals mandate deposition of sequencing data in SRA/GEO and processed data in supplementary tables. This is mostly followed in practice, and the data is actually usable (unlike in some other fields).
